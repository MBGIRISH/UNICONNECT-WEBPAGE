The Problem

College Life is Digitally Broken

Today's students are forced to glue together an unorganized web of generic platforms just to survive. The experience is noisy, unsafe, and fragmented.

📱

Scattered Conversations

Clubs are on Discord. Departmental announcements on Slack. Study chats on WhatsApp. Important events on Instagram. Essential communication is buried under endless notifications.

⚠️

Sketchy Marketplace Trading

Trading textbooks, dorm items, or tickets involves sketchy off-campus Facebook groups, meeting strangers in unlit parking lots, and endless scam bots. Zero security, infinite friction.

🔇

Invisible Campus Events

Half of the student body misses out on high-value guest lectures, club meetups, concert tickets, or networking hackathons because flyers are glued on dining hall walls or locked behind single groupchats.

🧊

Cold, Rigid Networking

Reaching out to peers for project collaborations, mentors for career tips, or alumni for internships feels unnatural. Cold LinkedIn outreach is stiff and returns a 2% reply rate.
